KELLY SONG: IN CONCERT

September 21st, 2024

Kelly concert day!!

The day had started out pretty interesting, actually. I had been practising driving with my dad in a parking lot for a while, when he suggested I drive us home. I knew he could tell I was getting a lot more comfortable on the road, and this was my next step, so I said yes. It was a pretty smooth ride over, actually, even though I was super stressed to see cars around me. I only got honked at once because apparently I took a split second too long at a left turn, but even my dad thinks that was an overreaction on the other driver's part.

Either way, I made it home without inflicting any damage on our car, and went to get ready for the concert.

Hayley picked me up from my place, and then we drove over to Emily's to get her and Lily. Only then did I realise that I forgot my ticket. As it turns out, though, Hayley is quite a smart cookie - she planned loads of time in advance, so we were able to go back to my place and still make it there on time.

When we arrived to Carleton, we parked, and Hayley and I (the parents) stood there for a bit, trying to figure out how the parking ticket machine worked, while Emily and Lily (the children) stood on the side, clueless.

Eventually, we got into the building, and met a few friends there. We decided to all sit together, and after a bit of waiting, the show had started.

When Kelly came on, I think it's safe to say we were all so proud of her - I know I was. She was so amazing, and throughout the few hours there, I constantly found myself thinking that I was really glad that I had come to her show. We had so much fun cheering her on, and when the show wrapped up, we all came to congratulate her and say our goodbyes.

Hayley, Emily, Lily and I hung around to take some pictures before heading back to Kanata for some food. We decided on Subway as our late-night meal spot, and yapped for a while while eating our food. I had a lot of fun talking with them, and I felt like we really connected.

By the time I came home, I felt fulfilled. It had been a really healing weekend, and I felt well-rested as I headed into the school week.
